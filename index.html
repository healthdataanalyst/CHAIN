Our Philosophy

Professional Training Over Academic Learning

After years of informal mentoring and career coaching, we've structured what works: professionals are built through daily execution in realistic environments, not passive content consumption.

01

Experience Over Credentials

A GitHub repository with working code proves capability. Certificates prove you watched videos. We build portfolio-grade deliverables that demonstrate real competence to employers.

02

Outcome-Focused Learning

Instead of "Learn SQL, Python, Power BI," we focus on "Build a vaccination surveillance system." Tools are learned in service of building systems, not as isolated subjects.

03

Systems Over Screenshots

Build end-to-end automated systems with data pipelines, clean models, production dashboards, and professional documentation—not disconnected exercises.

04

Professional Behavior

Communication, documentation, deadlines, collaboration—trained daily until automatic. We prepare you for how real teams operate with professional delivery standards.

05

Repetition Builds Mastery

One project teaches syntax. Three projects build fluency. We practice core competencies repeatedly with increasing complexity until you develop professional instinct.

06

Ambiguity Is The Curriculum

Real work is messy. Requirements are vague. Data is imperfect. We preserve realistic ambiguity so you develop judgment, not just execution capability.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to know Python, SQL, or Power BI before starting?

No! All technical skills are taught from scratch. Our instructor provides foundational learning materials for programming and Power BI. If you're completely new, expect to invest extra time beyond stated hours to learn basics in parallel. Biweekly one-on-one sessions help clarify doubts.

Do I need healthcare experience or knowledge?

No healthcare background required. We teach US healthcare systems (payer vs provider), EHR systems, population health concepts, terminology (HEDIS, FQHC, ICD-10), regulatory context (HIPAA, CMS), and how health departments operate—all integrated into project work.

What is the attendance requirement?

All programs require 85% or higher attendance at live sessions. This is strictly enforced to maintain professional training standards. Attendance below 85% may result in dismissal from the program without refund after Week 1.

How much time do I really need to commit?

Foundation Explorer: 18-20 hours/week. Professional Builder: 15-18 hours/week. Career Accelerator: 12-15 hours/week. If you're new to programming or tools, add 3-5 hours/week for foundational learning. All programs include live sessions and async work.

What if I get stuck or don't understand something?

You have biweekly one-on-one sessions with the instructor, async support via communication platform, starter code templates, and reference architectures. Getting stuck is part of learning—we're here to guide you through it.

Can I use this for college credit or internship?

Yes! Our Professional Builder program is designed to serve as a college capstone project or internship. You'll work with real government data from publicly available sources and build portfolio-ready systems. Many students receive academic credit—check with your institution.

Will this guarantee me a job?

We don't guarantee employment—no ethical training program can. But we provide what employers actually look for: real project experience, portfolio evidence, professional communication skills, domain knowledge, and hands-on system-building capability through structured, professional training.

Why is the pricing so economical compared to other programs?

Our training is priced at accessible rates to make professional development available to everyone—not for commercial profit. We use free and open-source tools wherever possible to keep costs low. Our mission: build exceptional talent through exceptional training. Every dollar goes toward mentorship and your development.

Which program should I choose?

Foundation Explorer: Testing if this approach works for you and learning healthcare basics. Professional Builder: Building your first strong portfolio piece and preparing for entry-level roles. Career Accelerator: Comprehensive professional readiness across multiple domains for mid-level positions.

What is the refund policy?

Before program start: Full refund up to 7 days before cohort start; 50% refund 3-6 days before; no refund within 48 hours. After program start: 50% refund if you withdraw within first 7 days (Week 1); no refunds after Week 1. One-time deferral to future cohort available for documented emergencies (valid up to 6 months).

How does the application process work?

Complete the application form carefully—applications are reviewed for seriousness and fit. Selected candidates receive further instructions via email within 3-5 business days. Application deadline is 7 days before cohort start. Fill applications thoughtfully to be considered.

Are programs available on-site for organizations?

Yes! While our standard delivery is online for global accessibility, we offer on-site training for corporate and academic partners upon request. Contact us for custom arrangements.
